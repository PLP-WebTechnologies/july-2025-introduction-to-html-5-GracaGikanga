KENYAN CUISINE

The Kenyan Cuisine is a detailed documentation of food and drinks from different Kenyan ethinicities. This raises awareness of the culture's traditions and helps to celebrate them.

Food Options:

  1. Nyama Choma
  2. Samaki wa Kupaka(Fish in Coconut rice)
  3. Mukimo
  4. Aliya (Preserved meat Dish)

Nyama Choma

Tribe: Kikuyu

Ingredients:

  • Goat or Beef
  • Salt
  • Lemon Juice
  • Chilli
  • Garlic

Recipe:

  1. Marinate meat with salt, lemon juice, chili, and garlic.
  2. Grill over open charcoal flame until tender and smoky.
  3. Serve with kachumbari (tomato-onion salad) and ugali.

Samaki wa Kupaka (Fish in coconut Rice)

Tribe: Coastal Community

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole fish
  • 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic & ginger paste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 tbsp oil

Recipe:

  1. Marinate Fish: Mix salt, turmeric, paprika, garlic, ginger, and lemon juice. Rub on fish. Let it rest 30 mins.
  2. Grill or Fry Fish: Cook until golden and done, about 5 to 7 minutes per side.
  3. Make Sauce: Saut√© onion in oil, add garlic, tomatoes, chili, and cook until soft. Add coconut milk. Simmer 5 to 10 mins. Add salt and lemon juice to taste.
  4. Combine: Place fish in sauce and simmer for 2 to 3 mins.

Mukimo

Tribe: Kikuyu Community

Ingredients:

  • 5 medium potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup maize kernels (boiled or pre-cooked)
  • 1 cup green peas or beans (pre-cooked)
  • 1 bunch of pumpkin leaves or spinach, finely chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil or margarine
  • 1 onion, chopped

Recipe:

  1. Boil Potatoes: Cook potatoes with salt until soft. Add greens in the last few minutes to soften.
  2. Add Peas/Maize: Mix in the pre-cooked peas and maize. Drain excess water.
  3. Mash Together: Mash everything until smooth and well mixed.
  4. Fry Onions: In a pan, fry onions in oil until golden, then stir into the mash.

Aliya (Preserved meat Dish)

Tribe: Luo Community

Ingredients:

  • 500g aliya (sun-dried beef), broken into small pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed (optional)
  • 1 tomato, chopped (optional)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp cooking oil or beef fat

Recipe:

  1. Soften the Meat: Boil the aliya in water for 30 to 45 minutes until tender. Add more water if needed.
  2. Fry Aromatics: In a pan, heat oil. Add onion (and garlic if using). Fry until golden.
  3. Add Tomatoes (Optional): Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Mix & Simmer: Add boiled aliya into the pan. Stir well and simmer for 5 to 10 minutes to blend flavors. Add a little water if dry.
